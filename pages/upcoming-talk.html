The Importance of Giant Molecular Clouds in Cosmic Ray Astrophysics

Giant molecular clouds (GMCs) are massive, dense areas of gas and dust in the Interstellar Medium (ISM) that are essential for creating stars and planets. These clouds also have a significant impact on cosmic ray (CR) astrophysics because of their potential to provide insights into the origins and nature of these High Energy (HE) particles. The origin of CRs can be confirmed by detecting non-thermal HE gamma-ray and neutrino emissions from the GMCs. Although both electrons and protons can produce HE gamma-rays through leptonic processes like Bremsstrahlung or Inverse Compton and hadronic processes like p-p interactions, neutrinos can only be created by hadronic processes (CR interaction with the target ISM material). Therefore, the detection of neutrinos is the key to revealing the CR sources. In the era of multi-messenger CR astrophysics, GMCs have become more crucial to pinpoint possible sources and accelerators of HE CR. In the presentation, I will discuss the interaction between CRs and GMCs using a combination of observational data, theoretical modelling, and Monte Carlo simulation.
