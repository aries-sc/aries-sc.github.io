Coronal Holes and their Detection: A simple overview

Coronal Holes are the darkest and least active regions in the solar corona as observed on the solar disk and above the solar limb. They are associated with high-speed solar winds that are responsible for a fraction of geomagnetic storms on Earth. Thus naturally its detection is crucial for predicting space weather. We will take a look at some popular detection methods and how Nueral Networks can help us take the field forward.
